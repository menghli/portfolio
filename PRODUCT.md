# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

UX/product design recruiters and hiring managers evaluating Menghan's research capabilities. Collaborators and peers in the design community. Context: typically browsing on desktop, reviewing 2–4 case studies per session, making a quick read-depth-signal judgment before committing to the full case study.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio that demonstrates Menghan Liang's UX research and product design craft. Success looks like a recruiter finishing a case study and coming away with a clear, confident read of Menghan's process and thinking — not just seeing polished visuals.

## Brand Personality

Precise, warm, unhurried. The site behaves the way a thoughtful designer presents work: structured but not rigid, detailed without being dense, confident without performing confidence.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-generated portfolio templates (Framer portfolio starters, cookie-cutter card grids)
- Over-animated Awwwards showcase sites that prioritize motion over readability
- SaaS landing-page aesthetics transplanted into a portfolio context

## Design Principles

1. **Reading comes first** — Every motion and layout choice must serve the reader, not interrupt them. Scroll reveals should feel like the page is quietly guiding attention, not performing.
2. **Restraint is craft** — One well-placed motion beats five scattered ones. The absence of decoration is a deliberate voice.
3. **Hierarchy through type, not decoration** — Weight, size, and spacing carry the hierarchy. No borders-as-decoration, no gradient text, no large icon-above-heading patterns.
4. **Consistent but not mechanical** — Pages share the same grammar but earn their individual rhythm based on content density and pacing.
5. **Show the thinking** — Design choices should feel legible: a visitor should understand *why* the page is laid out the way it is, not just admire that it looks clean.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. `prefers-reduced-motion` honored on every animation — transitions collapse to instant or crossfade. Color contrast on body text must exceed 4.5:1.
