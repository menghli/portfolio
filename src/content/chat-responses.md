## TELL ME ABOUT YOU

I'm Menghan (pronounced "Mong-han"), a UX researcher and product designer finishing my master's at the UW Information School. I studied Cognitive Science and HCI at Carnegie Mellon, where I first started thinking seriously about how people make sense of the things around them.

I've worked at Amazon, FUTU Holdings, ExpertVoice, Fundhomes, and Meyocks across enterprise IT, FinTech, e-commerce, real estate, and market research. Research came first, but watching how insights landed — or didn't — in design decisions made me want to close that gap myself.

Outside of work, I bake, crochet, hunt for good boba, and sing. Choirs and a cappella since middle school.

## WHAT DO YOU DESIGN?

Experiences that are easy to understand and hard to misuse. I care most about the moments where design either earns or loses someone's trust — confusing navigation, unclear feedback, friction that shouldn't exist.

Because I came up through research, I tend to spend more time than most designers asking why before asking what. That usually means starting messy, with lots of questions, and getting cleaner as the problem gets clearer.

---

## HOW DO YOU USE AI IN YOUR WORK?

As a thinking partner, not a shortcut. During my Amazon internship, I used AI to generate visual concepts for concept testing when no design resources were available — turning something abstract into something participants could actually react to. It changed the quality of the feedback entirely.

I'm interested in AI less as a tool for speed and more as a tool for expanding what's possible when you're working with constraints. The judgment about what matters still has to come from understanding people first.
